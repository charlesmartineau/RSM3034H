\begin{equation}
    \Delta Log(News\;count)_{i,t} = \beta_0 + \beta_1 1_{Ret<0, i,t} + \Gamma^{\prime}Controls_{i,t} + FE_{i,t} + \epsilon_{i,t}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    \Delta Log(News\;count)_{i,t} = \beta_0 + \beta_1 Ret^e_{i,t} + \beta_2 |Ret^e|_{i,t} + \Gamma^{\prime}Controls_{i,t} + FE_{i,t} + \epsilon_{i,t}
\end{equation}

\noindent $\Delta Log(News\;count)_{i,t}$ is the log change in the total number of news reported on time $t$ minus the rolling 60-day average of news for stock $i$ on date $t$. $Ret^e$ is the abnormal return, defined as the return minus the market return. $1_{Ret<0, i,t}$ is a dummy equal to 0 if $Ret^e<0$. $|Ret^e|$ is the absolute value of the abnormal return.  

$FE$ corresponds to industry, year-month fixed effects. I also incldue day of week FE.

The regression results presented below are reported for News count at time $t$ and $t+1$ (forecasting specifications).
